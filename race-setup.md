# Species Reference — D&D 2024 (5.5e)

---

## Aasimar

> *"Touched by the divine, marked by celestial purpose."*

Aasimar carry a spark of divine light within them — a blessing (and burden) passed down through bloodlines touched by angels, gods, or celestial planes. They may not always know their heritage, but others often sense something otherworldly about them: an uncanny calm, eyes that catch light strangely, or a warmth that makes the wounded recover faster in their presence. Aasimar are drawn to acts of compassion, justice, and protection, though some fall from grace and turn their divine gifts toward darker ends.

**Size:** Medium or Small (your choice)
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** 60 ft
**Languages:** Common + one other of your choice

### Traits

**Celestial Resistance**
You have Resistance to Necrotic damage and Radiant damage.

**Healing Hands**
As a Magic action, you touch a creature and roll a number of d4s equal to your Proficiency Bonus, restoring that many hit points. Once used, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

**Light Bearer**
You know the *Light* cantrip. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Celestial Revelation (Level 3)**
When you reach level 3, choose one of three forms to manifest when you activate this feature as a Bonus Action. It lasts 1 minute, can be used once per Long Rest, and deals bonus Radiant or Necrotic damage on your first attack each turn:
- *Heavenly Wings:* Grow luminous wings and gain a fly speed equal to your walking speed. Bonus damage: Radiant.
- *Inner Radiance:* Glow with brilliant light — each creature within 10 ft takes Radiant damage at the end of each of your turns. Bonus damage: Radiant.
- *Necrotic Shroud:* Black wings emerge and your eyes turn black — creatures that can see you within 10 ft must make a CHA save (DC = 8 + proficiency + CHA mod) or be Frightened until the end of their next turn. Bonus damage: Necrotic.

## Dragonborn

> *"Draconic blood runs hot — and it doesn't stay quiet."*

Dragonborn are proud, strong-willed beings who carry the literal blood of dragons. Whether descended from ancient draconic sorcerers, hatched from dragon eggs alongside humanoids, or born of some cosmic coupling, they bear unmistakeable dragon traits: scales, clawed hands, and a Breath Weapon that reflects the elemental power of their ancestral dragon. Dragonborn culture prizes honour, self-reliance, and excellence in one's chosen path.

**Size:** Medium
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** None (unless granted by Draconic Ancestry type)
**Languages:** Common + Draconic

### Traits

**Draconic Ancestry**
Choose one dragon type. This determines your Breath Weapon's damage type and your Damage Resistance:

| Dragon Type | Damage Type | Breath Shape |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| Amethyst | Force | Line (5×60 ft) |
| Black | Acid | Line (5×60 ft) |
| Blue | Lightning | Line (5×60 ft) |
| Brass | Fire | Line (5×60 ft) |
| Bronze | Lightning | Line (5×60 ft) |
| Copper | Acid | Line (5×60 ft) |
| Crystal | Radiant | Cone (15 ft) |
| Gold | Fire | Cone (15 ft) |
| Green | Poison | Cone (15 ft) |
| Red | Fire | Cone (15 ft) |
| Silver | Cold | Cone (15 ft) |
| White | Cold | Cone (15 ft) |

**Breath Weapon**
As a Bonus Action, exhale destructive energy. The size and shape is determined by your Draconic Ancestry. All creatures in the area must make a DEX saving throw (line) or CON saving throw (cone) against DC = 8 + proficiency + CON modifier. On a failed save, a creature takes 1d10 damage (of your ancestry's type), or half on a success. The damage increases to 2d10 at level 5, 3d10 at level 11, and 4d10 at level 17. You can use this a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, regained on a Long Rest.

**Damage Resistance**
You have Resistance to the damage type of your Draconic Ancestry.

## Dwarf

> *"Stone remembers. So do I."*

Dwarves are a proud, ancient people forged by the stone itself — short in stature but immense in endurance, determination, and craftsmanship. Their civilisations have stood for thousands of years, outlasting empires, and their racial memory is long and unforgiving. Dwarves treasure clan, craft, and the deep places of the world. Their Darkvision reaches twice as far as most races, their constitution resists poison, and their mystical connection to stone and metal makes them unparalleled artisans and miners.

**Size:** Medium or Small (your choice)
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** 120 ft
**Languages:** Common + Dwarvish

### Traits

**Dwarven Resilience**
You have Resistance to Poison damage and Advantage on saving throws made to avoid or end the Poisoned condition.

**Stonecunning**
As a Bonus Action, you gain Tremorsense 60 ft for 10 minutes. You must be standing on a stone surface or touching a stone surface to use this feature. You can use it a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, regained on a Long Rest.

**Tool Proficiency**
You gain proficiency with one of the following artisan's tools of your choice: Smith's Tools, Brewer's Supplies, or Mason's Tools.

## Elf

> *"Centuries pass like seasons. The world changes; we endure."*

Elves are ancient, graceful beings whose lifespans can stretch for centuries, giving them a perspective on history that most races can barely imagine. They emerged from the Feywild long ago and have never fully shed its dreamlike influence — their Trance replaces sleep with a meditative waking rest, and they are immune to the magical sleep that so easily claims humans. Elves are typically divided into lineages that define their particular gifts: High Elves are scholars and arcane practitioners, Wood Elves are swift hunters and rangers, and Drow (Dark Elves) are shaped by the Underdark.

**Size:** Medium or Small (your choice)
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** 60 ft
**Languages:** Common + Elvish

### Traits

**Fey Ancestry**
You have Advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the Charmed condition. Magic cannot put you to sleep.

**Keen Senses**
You gain proficiency in the Perception skill.

**Trance**
You don't need to sleep. Instead, you meditate deeply for 4 hours a day (the elven Trance), which counts as a full 8-hour Long Rest for you. You are partially aware of your surroundings and can wake instantly.

**Elven Lineage**
Choose one of the following lineages, which grants additional traits:

**High Elf Lineage**
*The arcane scholars, courtiers, and mages.*
- You know the *Prestidigitation* cantrip (INT is your spellcasting ability for it).
- At level 3, you learn *Detect Magic* and can cast it once per Long Rest without a spell slot.
- At level 5, you learn *Misty Step* and can cast it once per Long Rest without a spell slot.

**Wood Elf Lineage**
*The swift hunters and guardians of ancient forests.*
- Your walking speed increases to 35 ft.
- You know the *Druidcraft* cantrip (WIS is your spellcasting ability for it).
- At level 3, you learn *Longstrider* and can cast it once per Long Rest without a spell slot.
- At level 5, you learn *Pass Without Trace* and can cast it once per Long Rest without a spell slot.

**Drow Lineage**
*Born of the Underdark, shaped by darkness and politics.*
- Your Darkvision increases to 120 ft.
- You know the *Dancing Lights* cantrip (CHA is your spellcasting ability for it).
- At level 3, you learn *Faerie Fire* and can cast it once per Long Rest without a spell slot.
- At level 5, you learn *Darkness* and can cast it once per Long Rest without a spell slot.
- You have Disadvantage on attack rolls while you or your target is in direct sunlight (Sunlight Sensitivity).

## Gnome

> *"The world is full of mysteries. Fortunately, so am I."*

Gnomes are small, curious, and endlessly inventive — brimming with energy and enthusiasm for knowledge, tinkering, and magic. Their natural magical resistance makes them surprisingly durable despite their size, and their lineage determines whether they lean toward arcane invention, psychic abilities, or the ancient magic of the deep forest. Gnomes thrive in communities built on intellectual exchange and delightful eccentricity.

**Size:** Small
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** 60 ft
**Languages:** Common + Gnomish

### Traits

**Gnomish Cunning**
You have Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against magic.

**Gnomish Lineage**
Choose one of the following:

**Forest Gnome Lineage**
*Tricksters and friends of small animals.*
- You know the *Minor Illusion* cantrip (INT is your spellcasting ability for it).
- You can cast *Speak with Animals* using this trait. Once used, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest. You can also cast it by expending a spell slot.

**Rock Gnome Lineage**
*Tinkerers and inventors of clever mechanisms.*
- You gain proficiency with Artisan's Tools (Tinker's Tools specifically).
- Using those tools, you can spend 1 hour and 10gp of materials to create a Tiny clockwork device (a mechanical toy, fire starter, or music box). Up to 3 can exist at once; you can dismantle one as an Action to reclaim its parts.

**Deep Gnome (Svirfneblin) Lineage**
*Survivors of the Underdark, masters of camouflage.*
- Your Darkvision increases to 120 ft.
- You know the *Blindness/Deafness* cantrip (INT is your spellcasting ability for it).
- You can cast *Disguise Self* once per Long Rest using this trait (INT is spellcasting ability).

## Goliath

> *"The mountain does not move. Nor do I."*

Goliaths are enormous humanoids descended from the ancient giant bloodlines — standing well over 7 feet tall and built like granite cliffs. They hail from remote mountain peaks and frozen tundras where only the strongest survive. Their physical power and durability is extraordinary, and they carry an innate connection to the magic of giants. Goliath culture prizes competition, self-sufficiency, and respect earned through deeds rather than words.

**Size:** Medium
**Speed:** 35 ft
**Darkvision:** None
**Languages:** Common + Giant

### Traits

**Giant Ancestry**
You have a supernatural connection to the giants of old. Choose one of the following as your Giant Ancestry:
- *Cloud Giant:* Use reaction to impose Disadvantage on an attack against you (Misty Spawn). Uses: Proficiency Bonus per Long Rest.
- *Fire Giant:* When you hit with an attack, deal extra fire damage = Proficiency Bonus (Ember's Might). Uses: Proficiency Bonus per Long Rest.
- *Frost Giant:* When you hit with an attack, the target must make a CON save or have its speed reduced to 0 until the start of your next turn (Frost's Chill). Uses: Proficiency Bonus per Long Rest.
- *Hill Giant:* When you're reduced to 0 HP, gain temp HP equal to twice your Proficiency Bonus (Bulwark). Uses: Proficiency Bonus per Long Rest.
- *Stone Giant:* Throw a rock as an improvised weapon (1d10 + STR, 60 ft range) and knock prone on hit (Stone's Throw). Uses: Proficiency Bonus per Long Rest.
- *Storm Giant:* Swim speed = walking speed, and once per Long Rest cast *Lightning Bolt* (WIS is spellcasting ability) (Windseeker).

**Large Form**
Starting at level 5, as a Bonus Action you can become Large for 10 minutes (once per Long Rest). While Large, your reach increases by 5 ft and you deal 1d4 extra damage on Strength-based attacks. Your equipment doesn't change in size.

**Powerful Build**
You count as one size larger when determining carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

**Natural Athlete**
You have proficiency in the Athletics skill.

## Halfling

> *"Small doesn't mean helpless. It means overlooked — and that's an advantage."*

Halflings are small, cheerful folk who have made a virtue of being underestimated. Their Lucky trait makes them statistically harder to hit with bad luck than almost any other race — rerolling 1s on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws. They're nimble enough to slip through gaps in crowds and courageous enough to stand their ground when it counts. Halfling communities tend toward warmth, hospitality, and a quiet resistance to the assumption that bigger is better.

**Size:** Small
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** None
**Languages:** Common + one other of your choice

### Traits

**Brave**
You have Advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the Frightened condition.

**Halfling Nimbleness**
You can move through the space of any creature that is a size larger than you.

**Lucky**
When you roll a 1 on a d20 Test (attack roll, ability check, or saving throw), you can reroll the die and must use the new result.

**Naturally Stealthy**
You can take the Hide action when you are obscured by a creature that is at least one size larger than you.

## Human

> *"No heritage, no magic — just unlimited potential."*

Humans are the most widespread and adaptable species in most worlds. They lack the centuries-long memories of elves, the draconic power of Dragonborn, or the innate magic of Gnomes — but what they lack in specialised gifts they make up for in raw versatility. Humans progress faster, adapt to any culture, and can pick up skills and feats that other races simply don't start with. In the 2024 rules, Humans gain Heroic Inspiration after every Long Rest and start with an Origin Feat, making them genuinely competitive at level 1.

**Size:** Medium or Small (your choice)
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** None
**Languages:** Common + one other of your choice

### Traits

**Resourceful**
At the end of each Long Rest, you gain Heroic Inspiration if you don't already have it. (Heroic Inspiration can be spent to reroll any d20 Test once.)

**Skillful**
You gain proficiency in one additional skill of your choice.

**Versatile**
You gain one Origin Feat of your choice. Origin Feats are those granted by Backgrounds (Alert, Lucky, Skilled, Magic Initiate, etc.) — your human adaptability lets you start with an extra one.

## Orc

> *"I have already survived what should have killed me. What could you possibly do?"*

Orcs are powerfully built warriors shaped by a history of conflict and survival in harsh environments. They combine raw physical power with supernatural toughness — Relentless Endurance lets them refuse death once per Long Rest, and Adrenaline Rush lets them sprint past danger and regenerate temporary hit points mid-battle. In the 2024 rules, Orcs are fully rounded as a playable species with no alignment restrictions, representing fierce warriors, determined survivors, and powerful protectors.

**Size:** Medium
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** 120 ft
**Languages:** Common + Orc

### Traits

**Adrenaline Rush**
You can take the Dash action as a Bonus Action. When you do, you gain a number of Temporary Hit Points equal to your Proficiency Bonus. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Short or Long Rest.

**Powerful Build**
You count as one size larger when determining carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

**Relentless Endurance**
When you are reduced to 0 Hit Points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 HP instead. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until you finish a Long Rest.

## Tiefling

> *"They see the horns. They don't see what I've built despite them."*

Tieflings carry infernal, abyssal, or chthonic bloodlines that manifest in physical form — horns, tails, unusual skin colours, and eyes that glow in darkness. They are often feared, distrusted, or treated as omens of ill fortune, which has shaped Tiefling culture into one of fierce self-reliance and resilience. In the 2024 rules, Tieflings choose a Lineage that reflects their specific planar heritage — Abyssal, Chthonic, or Infernal — each granting different resistances and innate spellcasting.

**Size:** Medium or Small (your choice)
**Speed:** 30 ft
**Darkvision:** 60 ft
**Languages:** Common + one other of your choice

### Traits

**Otherworldly Presence**
You know the *Thaumaturgy* cantrip. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Tiefling Lineage**
Choose one of the following:

**Abyssal Lineage**
*Connected to the chaotic demons of the Abyss.*
- You have Resistance to Poison damage.
- You know *Poison Spray* cantrip (CHA is spellcasting ability).
- At level 3: Cast *Ray of Sickness* once per Long Rest without a slot.
- At level 5: Cast *Hold Person* once per Long Rest without a slot.

**Chthonic Lineage**
*Connected to the ancient powers of the underworld — death and shadow.*
- You have Resistance to Necrotic damage.
- You know *Chill Touch* cantrip (CHA is spellcasting ability).
- At level 3: Cast *False Life* once per Long Rest without a slot.
- At level 5: Cast *Ray of Enfeeblement* once per Long Rest without a slot.

**Infernal Lineage**
*The classic Tiefling — descended from devils and the Nine Hells.*
- You have Resistance to Fire damage.
- You know *Fire Bolt* cantrip (CHA is spellcasting ability).
- At level 3: Cast *Hellish Rebuke* once per Long Rest without a slot (as a 2nd-level spell).
- At level 5: Cast *Darkness* once per Long Rest without a slot.
