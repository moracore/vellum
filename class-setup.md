# Class Reference — D&D 2024 (5.5e)

---

## Barbarian

> *"Primal rage and an iron body — your fury is your armour."*

Barbarians are fierce warriors who tap into a primal power called Rage, allowing them to shrug off blows that would fell lesser fighters. They hail from wild frontiers, nomadic tribes, and brutal lands where survival depends on strength and ferocity. When a Barbarian enters Rage, they become a terrifying force of nature — stronger, harder to kill, and burning with a fury that dulls pain itself.

**Hit Die:** d12
**Primary Ability:** Strength
**Saving Throws:** Strength, Constitution
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour, Medium armour, Shields
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Martial weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** Unarmoured Defence — 10 + DEX modifier + CON modifier (no armour worn)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival

### Starting Equipment
- Greataxe OR any martial melee weapon
- 4 Handaxes OR any simple weapon
- Explorer's Pack
- 15 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Rage**
You can enter a Rage as a Bonus Action. While raging you gain: Advantage on STR checks and STR saving throws, a +2 bonus to damage rolls with STR-based attacks, and Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage. Rage lasts 1 minute and ends early if you don't attack a hostile creature or take damage. You can Rage 2 times per Long Rest (increases at higher levels).

**Unarmoured Defence**
While not wearing armour, your AC equals 10 + your DEX modifier + your CON modifier. You can still use a shield and benefit from this.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 Simple or Martial weapons you're proficient with. You can change these weapons after a Long Rest.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Path of the Berserker**
The primal path of pure rage. Berserkers can Frenzy during Rage — making a bonus attack as a Bonus Action each turn — and eventually become immune to charmed and frightened conditions. At higher levels, they can retaliate against attackers and grow so fearsome that creatures may not willingly approach.

**Path of the Wild Heart**
You channel the spirit of an animal, gaining supernatural senses and traits drawn from beasts. At level 3 you choose an animal aspect (Bear, Eagle, Elk, Wolf, etc.) that grants a unique benefit during Rage. You can also speak with animals and command beasts in battle.

**Path of the World Tree**
You draw power from Yggdrasil, the cosmic World Tree. Your Rage connects you to the tree's vast network — you can send your allies across the battlefield as bonus movement, grow reach with Vitality of the Tree temp HP, and eventually teleport between planes.

**Path of the Zealot**
Fuelled by divine fervour, Zealots deal bonus radiant or necrotic damage on their first attack each Rage. They are so devoted that dying barely slows them — they can keep fighting while at 0 HP during Rage, and allies can revive them without material components.

## Bard

> *"Words, music, and a sharp wit — the Bard's magic is the magic of inspiration."*

Bards are magical performers whose art draws on the weave of the universe itself. Whether wielding a lute in a tavern or whispering terrible truths to an enemy's mind, Bards blend magic and skill seamlessly. They are the ultimate generalists — capable healers, cunning spellcasters, and irreplaceable supports who elevate everyone around them with Bardic Inspiration.

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Charisma
**Saving Throws:** Dexterity, Charisma
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Hand crossbows, Longswords, Rapiers, Shortswords
**Tool Proficiencies:** 3 Musical instruments of your choice
**Starting AC:** 11 + DEX modifier (leather armour)

### Skill Choices — Pick 3 from ANY skill

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Charisma
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 2
- **Spells Known at Level 1:** 4 (from Bard spell list)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Known caster (learns specific spells, does not prepare from full list)

### Starting Equipment
- Leather Armour
- 2 Daggers
- Musical Instrument (one you're proficient with)
- Entertainer's Pack
- 15 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Bardic Inspiration**
As a Bonus Action, give one creature within 60 ft a Bardic Inspiration die (d6 at level 1). They can add it to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw within 10 minutes. You have a number of uses equal to your CHA modifier (minimum 1), regained on a Long Rest.

**Spellcasting**
You can cast Bard spells using Charisma as your spellcasting ability. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + CHA mod. Spell attack bonus = proficiency bonus + CHA mod.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**College of Dance**
Bards of Dance weave movement and magic together. Their footwork channels arcane energy — they can dash, disengage, and cast simultaneously. They gain Dexterity-based unarmed strikes and eventually teleport short distances as bonus actions using their dance.

**College of Glamour**
Drawing on the enchantments of the Feywild, these Bards can charm entire crowds, inspire followers with almost supernatural magnetism, and eventually project a Mantle of Majesty that compels creatures to obey simple commands.

**College of Lore**
Masters of secrets and cutting words, Lore Bards learn extra skills and gain Cutting Words — using a Bardic Inspiration die to subtract from an enemy's roll. They also steal spells from other classes via Magical Secrets at level 6.

**College of Valor**
Combat Bards who belong on the front line. They gain martial weapons, medium armour, and shield proficiency, can make an extra attack, and eventually fuse their spells into weapon strikes.

## Cleric

> *"Divine power flows through the devoted — the gods act through those who believe."*

Clerics are intermediaries between the mortal world and the divine. Granted power by their deity or divine concept, they wield holy magic to heal allies, smite foes, and alter the fabric of reality. Unlike most spellcasters, Clerics prepare their spells fresh each day from their entire class list, making them extraordinarily versatile. Their chosen Divine Order defines whether they heal the living, burn the undead, or embrace more shadowed paths.

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Wisdom
**Saving Throws:** Wisdom, Charisma
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour, Medium armour, Shields
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 16 (Chain Mail) OR 11 + DEX modifier + Shield (if light armour + shield)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
History, Insight, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Wisdom
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 3
- **Spells Prepared at Level 1:** WIS modifier + Cleric level (minimum 1)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Prepared caster (prepares from entire Cleric list each Long Rest)

### Starting Equipment
- Mace OR Warhammer (if proficient)
- Chain Mail OR Leather Armour + Shield
- Holy Symbol
- Priest's Pack
- 7 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Spellcasting**
Prepare spells from the entire Cleric list after a Long Rest. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + WIS mod.

**Divine Order**
At level 1, you choose your Divine Order (subclass), which grants you bonus spells always prepared, armour proficiency upgrades, or unique class features.

**Channel Divinity (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Use a pool of Channel Divinity charges to power domain-specific effects. All Clerics can Turn Undead.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 1 via Divine Order)

**Life Domain**
Dedicated to healing and the preservation of life. Life Clerics add extra hit points to their healing spells (Disciple of Life), can use Channel Divinity to fully restore a creature's HP, and eventually radiate an aura that heals nearby allies passively. They gain Heavy Armour proficiency.

**Light Domain**
Wielders of divine radiance and truth. Light Clerics gain the Warding Flare reaction (impose disadvantage on an attack), can flood a space with blinding holy light via Channel Divinity, and call down columns of holy fire at higher levels. Bonus spell: Faerie Fire, Burning Hands.

**Trickery Domain**
Gods of mischief and deception lend their Clerics illusions and deceptions. Trickery Clerics can Bless allies with the ability to make Stealth checks with advantage, create illusory duplicates of themselves, and eventually slip between shadows with Cloak of Shadows.

**War Domain**
Battle-hardened divine warriors. War Clerics gain martial weapons and Heavy Armour, can use Channel Divinity to boost their attack rolls significantly, and gain multiple attacks at higher levels. They are among the most combat-capable Clerics.

**Knowledge Domain**
Servants of gods of knowledge and memory. These Clerics can briefly gain proficiency in any skill, read minds, and eventually learn any language or skill instantly. They excel at information gathering and utility.

## Druid

> *"The natural world remembers everything — and the Druid speaks its language."*

Druids draw power from the living world itself — the turning of seasons, the cycles of life and death, and the primal force that flows through every root, river, and storm cloud. They are shapeshifters, heralds of nature's fury, and wise custodians of the wild. Druids prepare their spells from a broad list of nature magic and can call lightning as easily as they mend wounds.

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Wisdom
**Saving Throws:** Intelligence, Wisdom
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour, Medium armour, Shields (no metal armour)
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Clubs, Daggers, Darts, Javelins, Maces, Quarterstaves, Scimitars, Sickles, Slings, Spears
**Tool Proficiencies:** Herbalism Kit
**Starting AC:** 13 + min(DEX modifier, 2) (scale mail) OR 11 + DEX modifier (leather)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, Survival

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Wisdom
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 2
- **Spells Prepared at Level 1:** WIS modifier + Druid level (minimum 1)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Prepared caster (prepares from entire Druid list each Long Rest)

### Starting Equipment
- Leather Armour
- Shield
- Scimitar OR any simple weapon
- Druidic Focus (staff, totem, etc.)
- Explorer's Pack
- 9 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Druidic**
You know Druidic, the secret language of Druids. You can speak it and use it to leave hidden messages. Only other Druids can decode these messages automatically.

**Spellcasting**
Prepare spells from the Druid list after a Long Rest. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + WIS mod.

**Wild Shape (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Transform into beasts you have seen. At level 2 you can become creatures up to CR 1/4 with no swim or fly speed.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Circle of the Land**
Druids tied to a specific terrain (Arctic, Coast, Desert, Forest, Grassland, Mountain, Swamp, Underdark) gain bonus spells from that terrain and recover expended spell slots during Short Rests. They are the most powerful pure spellcasters among Druid subclasses.

**Circle of the Moon**
Masters of Wild Shape. Moon Druids can transform into more powerful beasts (CR 1 at level 3, scaling upward), take on elemental forms at level 10, and eventually become a permanent shapeshifter. Excellent for frontline combat.

**Circle of the Sea**
Bound to the ocean and storms. Sea Druids gain bonus thunder/lightning/cold spells, can wreath themselves in a Warding Wind, and channel the wrath of the deep — crashing waves on enemies and summoning tidal power in combat.

**Circle of the Stars**
Cosmic Druids map the stars and channel their light. They gain a Starry Form (choose Archer for radiant bolts, Chalice for healing, Dragon for concentration protection), and eventually call the stars themselves down as damage.

## Fighter

> *"Discipline, training, and an iron will — the Fighter is the master of the battlefield."*

Fighters are the consummate warriors — trained in all armour, all weapons, and all the tactical tools of war. What they lack in magic they more than make up for with raw fighting ability and resilience. Every Fighter chooses a Fighting Style to specialise their combat, and gains Action Surge — the ability to take an additional action in combat — making them devastatingly effective when it counts.

**Hit Die:** d10
**Primary Ability:** Strength or Dexterity
**Saving Throws:** Strength, Constitution
**Armour Proficiencies:** All armour, Shields
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Martial weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 16 (Chain Mail) OR 11 + DEX modifier (leather + longbow build)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Acrobatics, Animal Handling, Athletics, History, Insight, Intimidation, Perception, Survival

### Starting Equipment
- Chain Mail OR Leather Armour + Longbow + 20 Arrows
- Martial weapon + Shield OR 2 Martial weapons
- Light Crossbow + 20 bolts OR 2 Handaxes
- Dungeoneer's Pack
- 4 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Fighting Style**
Choose one specialisation:
- *Archery:* +2 bonus to ranged weapon attack rolls.
- *Defence:* +1 to AC while wearing armour.
- *Dueling:* +2 damage when wielding a one-handed weapon in one hand only.
- *Great Weapon Fighting:* Reroll 1s and 2s on damage dice for two-handed/versatile weapons.
- *Protection:* Use reaction to impose Disadvantage on an attack against an adjacent ally (requires shield).
- *Two-Weapon Fighting:* Add ability modifier to the damage of your off-hand attack.
- *Blind Fighting:* Gain Blindsight 10 ft.
- *Thrown Weapon Fighting:* Draw thrown weapons as part of the attack, +2 damage with thrown weapons.
- *Unarmed Fighting:* Unarmed strikes deal 1d6 (or 1d8 with no other weapon), and grapple targets take 1d4 bludgeoning at start of their turn.
- *Interception:* Use reaction to reduce damage to an adjacent creature by 1d10 + proficiency bonus.

**Second Wind**
As a Bonus Action, regain 1d10 + Fighter level HP. Usable once per Short or Long Rest (2 uses at level 2).

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 3 weapons you're proficient with (more than any other class).

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Battle Master**
The tactical genius. Battle Masters learn Manoeuvres — powerful combat techniques fuelled by Superiority Dice (d8s). Manoeuvres include Disarming Strike, Precision Attack, Rally (grant temp HP), Trip Attack, and many more. They have the highest skill ceiling of Fighter subclasses.

**Champion**
The straightforward powerhouse. Champions gain an expanded critical hit range (crit on 19–20 at level 3, 18–20 at level 15), a second Fighting Style, and exceptional athletic ability. Excellent for players who want reliable, no-fuss damage.

**Eldritch Knight**
Fighters who supplement their martial prowess with Wizard magic. They learn a limited selection of Wizard spells (mostly Abjuration and Evocation) and can bond a weapon to them magically, summoning it back as a Bonus Action. At level 7 they can use War Magic to attack after casting a cantrip.

**Psi Warrior**
Psychically augmented warriors who generate Psionic Energy Dice (d6s) to fuel their abilities. They can push enemies back, form protective shields, and eventually levitate and project their senses. A solid balance of offence and defence.

## Monk

> *"The body is the weapon. The mind is the armour."*

Monks are disciplined martial artists who have trained their bodies to channel ki — a mystical energy found in all living things. Unarmed and lightly armoured, they are nonetheless terrifyingly fast, able to deflect missiles, sprint up walls, and stun opponents with precise strikes. Each Monastic Tradition shapes how a Monk channels their ki, from shadow-stepping assassins to elemental conjurers.

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Strength or Dexterity
**Saving Throws:** Strength, Dexterity
**Armour Proficiencies:** None
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Shortswords
**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of artisan's tools OR one musical instrument
**Starting AC:** Unarmoured Defence — 10 + DEX modifier + WIS modifier

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, Stealth

### Starting Equipment
- Shortsword OR any simple weapon
- Dungeoneer's Pack
- 5 Darts

### Level 1 Features

**Martial Arts**
Your unarmed strikes and Monk weapons (simple weapons + shortswords) can use DEX instead of STR for attack and damage rolls. Your unarmed strike damage die is 1d6 (increases at higher levels). When you attack with a Monk weapon or unarmed strike, you can make one unarmed strike as a Bonus Action.

**Unarmoured Defence**
While not wearing armour or using a shield, your AC = 10 + DEX modifier + WIS modifier.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

**Ki (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* You gain Ki points equal to your Monk level, spent on features like Flurry of Blows, Patient Defence, and Step of the Wind.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Warrior of the Open Hand**
The purest expression of Monk combat. Open Hand Monks can knock enemies prone, push them back, or deny their reactions using Flurry of Blows. They also gain Wholeness of Body (heal yourself once per Long Rest) and eventually become immune to disease and poison.

**Warrior of Shadow**
Shadow Monks who use ki to blend into darkness. They can cast Darkness, Darkvision, Silence, and Pass Without Trace with ki points, and at level 6 they can teleport between shadows as a Bonus Action. Excellent for stealth-focused playstyles.

**Warrior of the Elements**
Masters of elemental power who reshape their ki into fire, water, earth, and air. They can change damage types of their attacks, summon elemental waves, and eventually walk on water and gain a fly speed.

**Warrior of Mercy**
Healer-warriors who blend combat with restoration. Mercy Monks can Flurry of Healing (spend ki to restore HP during Flurry of Blows), apply Hand of Harm to deal necrotic damage, and eventually cure diseases and poisons with their touch.

## Paladin

> *"A sacred oath is a weapon as powerful as any sword."*

Paladins are holy warriors bound by a sacred oath that grants them divine magic and supernatural resilience. They combine the combat power of a Fighter with the healing and buffing magic of a Cleric, and their Aura of Protection — which adds their CHA modifier to all saving throws for nearby allies — makes them one of the most impactful party members in the game. Their oath defines their flavour, spell list, and special Channel Divinity abilities.

**Hit Die:** d10
**Primary Ability:** Strength and Charisma
**Saving Throws:** Wisdom, Charisma
**Armour Proficiencies:** All armour, Shields
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Martial weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 18 (Chain Mail + Shield)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Athletics, Insight, Intimidation, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Charisma
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 0
- **Spells Prepared at Level 1:** CHA modifier + half Paladin level (rounded up, minimum 1)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Prepared caster (prepares from Paladin list + Oath spells always prepared)

### Starting Equipment
- Chain Mail
- Shield
- Martial weapon (longsword, warhammer, etc.)
- 5 Javelins
- Holy Symbol
- Priest's Pack
- 9 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Lay on Hands**
A pool of HP equal to 5 × Paladin level. As an Action, touch a creature to restore any amount from this pool. Replenished on a Long Rest.

**Spellcasting**
Prepare Paladin spells + Oath spells. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + CHA mod.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

**Divine Smite**
When you hit with a weapon attack, you can expend a spell slot to deal extra Radiant damage: 2d8 per slot level (3d8 vs undead/fiends).

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3 — Oath)

**Oath of Devotion**
The classic paladin — a beacon of honour and justice. Devotion Paladins gain bonus spells (Protection from Evil and Good, Sanctuary, etc.) and Channel Divinity options including Sacred Weapon (+CHA to attacks) and Turn the Unholy. Their capstone makes them permanently blessed.

**Oath of the Ancients**
Guardians of life, light, and the natural world. Ancient Paladins gain spells like Ensnaring Strike and Misty Step. Their Aura at level 7 grants resistance to spell damage to nearby allies, making them exceptional against casters.

**Oath of Glory**
Champions who inspire greatness in others. Glory Paladins gain spells that boost speed and movement and can use Channel Divinity to grant nearby allies Advantage on Strength checks/saves or teleport them out of danger.

**Oath of Vengeance**
Relentless hunters of the wicked. Vengeance Paladins specialise in locking down a single target — Vow of Enmity grants Advantage against one creature, and they gain spells like Hold Person and Misty Step. They are the most offensively focused Paladin subclass.

## Ranger

> *"The wilderness is both home and hunting ground."*

Rangers are expert hunters and survivalists, equally at home tracking prey through dense forest or sniping from a clifftop. They combine martial skill with nature magic and an unparalleled knowledge of specific terrains and prey. In the 2024 rules, Rangers gain full spellcasting from level 1 and their Favoured Enemy and Natural Explorer features are significantly more flexible and powerful than in previous editions.

**Hit Die:** d10
**Primary Ability:** Dexterity and Wisdom
**Saving Throws:** Strength, Dexterity
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour, Medium armour, Shields
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Martial weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 13 + min(DEX modifier, 2) (scale mail) OR 11 + DEX modifier (leather)

### Skill Choices — Pick 3
Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, Survival

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Wisdom
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 0
- **Spells Known at Level 1:** 2 (from Ranger spell list)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Known caster (learns fixed spells, doesn't prepare from full list)

### Starting Equipment
- Scale Mail OR Leather Armour
- 2 Shortswords OR 2 Simple Melee Weapons
- Shortbow + 20 Arrows OR Longbow + 20 Arrows
- Dungeoneer's Pack
- 7 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Favoured Enemy**
You always have Hunter's Mark prepared (doesn't count against spells known) and can cast it once per Long Rest without expending a spell slot.

**Spellcasting**
Know spells from the Ranger list. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + WIS mod.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

**Deft Explorer (Level 1)**
You gain Expertise in one skill you're proficient with. Also gain a natural explorer terrain benefit.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Gloom Stalker**
Hunters of the dark — perfectly designed for dungeon crawls and ambushes. Gloom Stalkers are invisible to darkvision, gain a bonus to Initiative, and make an extra attack on the first turn of combat. They're also immune to magical darkness.

**Hunter**
The classic Ranger focused on eliminating prey. Hunters choose from a menu of offensive options at levels 3, 7, 11, and 15: Colossus Slayer (extra damage against wounded foes), Horde Breaker (extra attack against adjacent enemy), Escape the Horde (impose Disadvantage on opportunity attacks), etc.

**Beast Master**
Rangers who bond with an animal companion that fights alongside them. The Primal Beast companion acts on the Ranger's turn and scales with the Ranger's proficiency bonus, solving the classic companion action-economy problem.

**Fey Wanderer**
Rangers touched by the Feywild who blend enchantment magic with their hunting skills. They add CHA modifier to damage rolls, can charm or frighten creatures, and gain access to illusion and teleportation spells like Misty Step.

## Rogue

> *"Strike once, strike true — and never be seen."*

Rogues are specialists in precision and cunning. Their Sneak Attack ability means a single well-placed blow can deal enormous damage, and their Cunning Action lets them Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a Bonus Action — keeping them mobile and evasive. More than just assassins, Rogues are also the party's faces, scouts, and thieves, with Expertise doubling their proficiency bonus in their chosen skills.

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Dexterity
**Saving Throws:** Dexterity, Intelligence
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons, Hand crossbows, Longswords, Rapiers, Shortswords
**Tool Proficiencies:** Thieves' Tools
**Starting AC:** 11 + DEX modifier (leather armour)

### Skill Choices — Pick 4
Acrobatics, Athletics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, Stealth

### Starting Equipment
- Rapier OR Shortsword
- Shortbow + 20 Arrows OR Shortsword
- Leather Armour
- 2 Daggers
- Thieves' Tools
- Burglar's Pack
- 8 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Sneak Attack**
Once per turn, deal extra 1d6 damage to a target if you have Advantage on the attack OR an ally is adjacent to the target (and you don't have Disadvantage). The extra damage increases by 1d6 every odd level.

**Thieves' Cant**
You know a secret mix of slang, signs, and ciphers used by rogues and criminals. You can convey secret messages to other Thieves' Cant speakers hidden in normal conversation.

**Expertise**
Double your proficiency bonus for 2 skills (or 1 skill + Thieves' Tools). You gain 2 more at level 6.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

**Cunning Action (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Take Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a Bonus Action each turn.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Thief**
Fast and nimble opportunists. Thieves gain Fast Hands (use Thieves' Tools or sleight of hand as Bonus Action), Second-Story Work (climbing speed, jump distance boost), and eventually the legendary Supreme Sneak and Use Magic Device features.

**Assassin**
Specialists in eliminating targets before they know a fight has begun. Assassins gain proficiency in disguise and poisoner's kits, automatically crit against surprised enemies (Assassinate), and can create perfect disguises and fake identities.

**Arcane Trickster**
Rogues who dabble in Wizard magic — specifically illusions and enchantments. They can cast spells, deliver them via Mage Hand Legerdemain, and eventually steal spells from enemy casters.

**Soulknife**
Psychic Rogues who manifest blades of psionic energy. They need no weapons — their Psychic Blades appear and vanish at will and deal psychic damage. They also gain telepathy, can read surface thoughts, and teleport short distances.

**Phantom**
Rogues who straddle the line between life and death. Phantoms can steal slivers of souls from dying creatures (Tokens of the Departed) to use for bonus damage or proficiency, and they eventually gain a spectral form that grants flight and resistance.

## Sorcerer

> *"Magic doesn't flow through you. It *is* you."*

Sorcerers carry magic in their blood — inherited from dragons, cosmic forces, divine bloodlines, or wild chaos. Unlike Wizards who study, Sorcerers simply *are* magic. They have fewer spells but can shape and enhance them using Sorcery Points and Metamagic — twinning spells to hit two targets, silently casting in anti-magic zones, empowering damage rolls, or casting spells as Bonus Actions. In the right hands, a Sorcerer is extraordinarily powerful.

**Hit Die:** d6
**Primary Ability:** Charisma
**Saving Throws:** Constitution, Charisma
**Armour Proficiencies:** None
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 10 + DEX modifier (no armour — Mage Armour spell gives 13 + DEX)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, Religion

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Charisma
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 4
- **Spells Known at Level 1:** 2 (from Sorcerer spell list)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Known caster (fixed spells known, cannot swap freely)

### Starting Equipment
- Light Crossbow + 20 Bolts OR 2 Daggers
- Arcane Focus
- Dungeoneer's Pack
- 8 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Spellcasting**
Cast Sorcerer spells using CHA. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + CHA mod.

**Innate Sorcery**
As a Bonus Action, you can unleash the magic within yourself for 1 minute. While active, your spells ignore resistance, and you gain Advantage on the attack rolls of Sorcerer spells. Usable twice per Long Rest.

**Font of Magic (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Gain Sorcery Points (equal to Sorcerer level) to power Metamagic and convert to/from spell slots.

**Metamagic (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Modify your spells with options like Twinned Spell, Quickened Spell, Subtle Spell, Empowered Spell, etc.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3)

**Aberrant Mind**
Sorcerers touched by eldritch entities from beyond reality. They gain bonus psionic spells (telepathy, mind blast effects), can communicate telepathically, and reshape their physical form in unsettling ways. Their spells emphasise psychic damage and mental manipulation.

**Clockwork Soul**
Infused with the order of the plane of Mechanus. Clockwork Sorcerers can cancel Advantage or Disadvantage on any roll (Restore Balance), gain spells focused on protection and law, and eventually become immune to the charmed and frightened conditions.

**Draconic Bloodline**
The most iconic Sorcerer. You carry dragon blood and gain Draconic Resilience (AC 13 + DEX without armour, +1 HP per level), elemental affinity (bonus damage of your dragon's type), and eventually sprout wings for flight.

**Wild Magic**
Raw, unpredictable power that occasionally misfires. Wild Magic Sorcerers can trigger Wild Magic Surges — random chaotic effects from the Wild Magic table — but also Bend Luck (force +/-1d4 on any roll nearby), and harness the chaos for enormous power bursts.

## Warlock

> *"Power has a price. The Warlock has already paid it."*

Warlocks forge pacts with powerful entities — Archfey, Demons, Great Old Ones, or Celestials — to gain eldritch power. Their magic is unique: they have very few spell slots (Pact Magic) that recharge on a Short Rest rather than a Long Rest, and they learn their patron's secrets through Eldritch Invocations — dozens of customisable abilities that reshape how their magic works. Warlocks are defined by their patron and the deals they've made.

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Charisma
**Saving Throws:** Wisdom, Charisma
**Armour Proficiencies:** Light armour
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 11 + DEX modifier (leather armour)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Arcana, Deception, History, Intimidation, Investigation, Nature, Religion

### Spellcasting (Pact Magic)
- **Ability:** Charisma
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 2
- **Spells Known at Level 1:** 2 (from Warlock spell list)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 1 (recovers on Short OR Long Rest)
- **Type:** Known caster, Pact Magic slots

### Starting Equipment
- Light Crossbow + 20 Bolts OR any simple weapon
- Leather Armour
- Arcane Focus
- 2 Daggers
- Dungeoneer's Pack OR Scholar's Pack
- 15 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Spellcasting (Pact Magic)**
Pact Magic slots recharge on Short or Long Rest. At level 1 you have 1 slot of level 1. All Warlock spells are cast at the highest available slot level.

**Eldritch Invocations (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Learn 2 Invocations from a large list. Options include: Agonising Blast (add CHA to Eldritch Blast damage), Armour of Shadows (Mage Armour at will), Devil's Sight (darkvision through magical darkness), Repelling Blast (push 10 ft on hit), and many more.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 3 — Patron)

**The Archfey**
A pact with a capricious lord of the Feywild. Archfey Warlocks gain illusion and enchantment powers — they can Beguiling Defences (reflect charms back), Step Into the Feywild (teleport, turn invisible, gain resistance), and access spells like Misty Step and Faerie Fire.

**The Celestial**
A pact with a being of divine light — an angel or solar. Celestial Warlocks gain healing abilities (Healing Light pool), access to Cleric spells like Cure Wounds and Revivify, and eventually project a radiant soul that damages nearby enemies.

**The Fiend**
The iconic Warlock — a pact with a devil or demon. Fiend Warlocks gain Temporary HP whenever they kill a creature (Dark One's Blessing), access to fire and necromancy spells, and eventually become immune to fire damage and resist non-magical weapons.

**The Great Old One**
A pact with an alien cosmic entity beyond human understanding. Great Old One Warlocks can communicate telepathically, read surface thoughts, project madness into other minds, and gain access to psychic and mind-altering spells. They eventually create a mental link with their entire party.

## Wizard

> *"Given enough time and study, magic has no limits."*

Wizards are the ultimate arcane scholars — methodical students of magic who learn spells by transcribing them into their spellbook. While they are physically fragile, a Wizard's spell list is the broadest of any class, spanning every school of magic. They prepare spells each day from their spellbook, can scribe new spells from scrolls, and use Arcane Recovery to regain spell slots mid-day. Their Arcane Tradition (subclass) determines their area of specialisation.

**Hit Die:** d6
**Primary Ability:** Intelligence
**Saving Throws:** Intelligence, Wisdom
**Armour Proficiencies:** None
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Simple weapons
**Tool Proficiencies:** None
**Starting AC:** 10 + DEX modifier (no armour — Mage Armour gives 13 + DEX)

### Skill Choices — Pick 2
Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Religion

### Spellcasting
- **Ability:** Intelligence
- **Cantrips at Level 1:** 3
- **Spells in Spellbook at Level 1:** 6 (you choose these; your spellbook holds them)
- **Spells Prepared at Level 1:** INT modifier + Wizard level (from your spellbook)
- **Level 1 Spell Slots:** 2
- **Type:** Prepared caster from personal spellbook

### Starting Equipment
- Quarterstaff OR Dagger
- Arcane Focus
- Scholar's Pack
- Spellbook (contains your 6 starting spells)
- 4 gp

### Level 1 Features

**Spellcasting**
Prepare spells from your spellbook. Spell save DC = 8 + proficiency + INT mod. You can copy new spells into your spellbook from spell scrolls or other Wizards' books (2 hours + 50gp per spell level).

**Arcane Recovery**
Once per Long Rest, after a Short Rest, regain expended spell slots whose combined levels equal up to half your Wizard level (rounded up). At level 1 this means recovering a single level-1 slot.

**Weapon Mastery**
You can use the Mastery property of 2 weapons you're proficient with.

**Ritual Adept**
You can cast any spell you know as a ritual if it has the ritual tag (takes 10 minutes extra, no slot required).

**Scholar (Level 2)**
*(Available from level 2)* Choose your Arcane Tradition subclass, and gain Advantage on INT checks using Arcana or History to recall information about your tradition's focus.

### Subclasses (chosen at Level 2 via Arcane Tradition)

**Abjuration**
Masters of protection and warding. Abjuration Wizards create an Arcane Ward (temporary HP absorbs damage, recharged by casting abjuration spells) and eventually project their ward onto allies and banish creatures from other planes.

**Conjuration**
Specialists in calling things into being. Conjuration Wizards can Minor Conjuration (create a small non-magical object briefly), teleport short distances (Benign Transposition), and eventually Focused Conjuration (concentration on conjuration spells can't be broken by damage).

**Divination**
Seers who peer into the future. Divination Wizards gain Portent — they roll 2d20 at each dawn and can replace any attack roll, save, or ability check with one of these rolls. They also gain the ability to ask cosmic questions and eventually foresee all possible outcomes.

**Evocation**
Pure power casters who sculpt destructive magic. Evocation Wizards can exclude allies from area spells (Sculpt Spells), add INT modifier to damage on cantrips (Potent Cantrip), and eventually empower their spells to deal maximum damage once per Long Rest.

**Illusion**
Masters of deception and misdirection. Illusion Wizards can Malleable Illusions (alter concentration illusions as an Action), create a near-real Illusory Self as a reaction to avoid an attack, and eventually create illusions so perfect they can actually harm creatures.

**Necromancy**
Wielders of the power of death. Necromancy Wizards add their proficiency bonus to Necrotic damage, reap HP from enemies when they kill them (Grim Harvest), and can raise undead that are stronger than normal and under complete control.

**Transmutation**
Shapers of matter and form. Transmutation Wizards create a Transmuter's Stone that grants one permanent benefit (Darkvision, extra movement, resistance, etc.) and can change one material into another as a higher-level feature.

**Bladesinging**
*(Traditionally from the Forgotten Realms)* Elven Wizard tradition that merges spellcasting with swordplay. Bladesingers activate Bladesong to add INT to AC and speed, gain Advantage on Acrobatics, and can make one weapon attack as a Bonus Action when casting a spell.
